Equitable Health Care Access

How can we ensure that all class citizens will receive equitable access to health care?

THE TRUTH

Despite the growing number of citizens with health insurance and coverage in California, those of lower class and minority groups are not receiving equitable accessibility to health care; in particular, recipients of our state issued Medi-Cal program are experiencing the most difficulty in accessing necessary health care. 

20%
of Californians with Medi-Cal are having difficulties finding health care providers who will accept their insurance plan.
    “Survey Says: Californians’ Perspectives on Health Care,” Perry Undem Research/ Communication, California Health Care Foundation, March 2015, Dec 28, 2016

    43%
    of adults in fair/poor health reported to have more difficulty receiving neccessary health care from specialists due to high costs.
    “Survey Says: Californians’ Perspectives on Health Care,” Perry Undem Research/ Communication, California Health Care Foundation, March 2015, Dec 28, 2016

    77%
    of Medi-Cal recipients reported to want more time to talk with doctors concerning questions and receiving help.

        Our Solution

        Health is everything. And if everyone can't have it, well, then what's the point? Everyone needs health care, and the meeting of that need should not be dependent on whether or not you can afford it. 

                                                                                                                                                                                            -Nora Evison (2017)

        We will work closely with our very own Covered California to enact substantial health care policy changes that will require that all health care plan providers to aid recipients in the process of receiving health care.  One such plan includes direcly plugging recipients to a primary care physician within a certain period of time (e.g. 60 days). 


        This new policy/rule would include all recipients of Medi-Cal receiving mandatory aid in being directed to consistent health care providers best suited for their needs and budgets and eliminate the challenges Medi-Cal recipients have had to face in finding a healthcare provider who accepts Medi-Cal by themselves. It would directly introduce people to someone familiar with the domain of healthcare who can guide them in finding the right medical assistance and satisfy many (esp. Medi-Cal) patients’ desire to have more time and ability to discuss their healthcare situations with a physician.
