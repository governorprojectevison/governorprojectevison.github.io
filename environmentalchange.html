Environmental Change

What can we do to make our energy extracting practices more environmentally friendly?

FRACKING

One of the most environmentally abusive yet widely used
energy extraction methods in California today.

In California, we are not being as energy efficient as we should; specifically, allowing fracking on our fault lines and off the coast and underutilizing many cleaner options for energy. We are one of the greener states, but there is always room for improvement.

California is prime fracking land due to the fact that we lay on the San Andreas Fault Line. However, much more fracking also takes place in our most delicate ecosystem, off the coast of California.

Fracking has been reported in about a sixth of California counties: Colusa, Glenn, Kern, Los Angeles, Monterey, Sacramento, Santa Barbara, Sutter, Kings and Ventura.

Fracking causes the dispersion of hazardous chemicals into the groundwater and air, which leads to the destruction of many of our precious flora and fauna's homes and habitats.

With the support of organizations, such as Green Plus, a clean, fracking-free California will soon be underway! Green Plus seeks to create energy sustainable platforms for small and medium businesses that will enable the average citizen to play a pivotal part in creating environmental change in California.


Costs are not only manageable, ranging from only $75-$275 per year, but members are sometimes also offered ‘scholarships’ where businesses can be serviced for zero charge. They are available for even very small businesses with as few as two employees. 87% of businesses claim that sustainability has become more integrated into their organization since joining Green Plus.
